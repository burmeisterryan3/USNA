To sort numbers with a skip list, one can simply insert numbers into the list.
The resulting list will be in order and to find an element one simply needs to
perform the usual search algorithm.  The cost of the algorithm then becomes the
cost of insertion for $n$ elements.  The cost of insertion for each element is
simply $\mathcal{O}\left(log n\right)$.  Thefore, the total cost of the
algorithm is $\mathcal{O}\left(nlog n\right)$.
