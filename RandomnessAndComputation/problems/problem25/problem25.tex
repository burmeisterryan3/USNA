\begin{center}
Lehmer LCG Equation \\
$x_i \leftarrow x_{i-1}*\ a\ \text{mod} m$
\end{center}

When $m=65537$, a parameter $a$ that maximizes the period is $3$.  This can be
proven by showing the following: 

\begin{center}
$a^d\equiv 1\ \text{mod} m$,
 $a$ is a primitive root modulo $m$ (i.e. their greatest divisor is 1),
and $d$ is the smallest number for which the above is true.
\end{center}

When each of the above is true, the period will max out at a length of $m-1$.
When $a=3$, the smallest number which satisifies the equation above is $65536$,
and as $65536=m-1$, I know that I have found an ideal set of parameters of rhte
Lehmer LCG.
